How is Malaysia's vaccination program doing?

Like the rest of the world, Malaysians long to return to normality. The only way to make this a reality is through vaccination. As Australia has proven, the virus is here to stay. We will have to learn to live with it.

Let us take a look at how Malaysia is doing in this aspect.

Vaccination Progress by State as of 31 August 2021

In general, the west coast states and Sarawak seem to have made better progress in vaccinating its residents, surpassing 50% inoculation rate in both administering one and two doses.

Both Sabah and Kelantan seem to progress similarly. However, they appear to be the most behind in administering both one (41%) and two doses (29%).

% of Total Population Fully Vaccinated and Partially Vaccinated
as of 31 August 2021

Although Malaysia's vaccination rollout started off slowly, it picked up quite fast. As of 31st August 2021, 47.4% of Malaysia's population has been fully vaccinated while 62.1% are partially vaccinated.

According to Malaysia's National Security Council, an individual is fully vaccinated against Covid-19 14 days after receiving the second dose of Pfizer, Astrazeneca or Sinovac vaccines; or 28 days after a single dose of Johnson & Johnson or Cansino vaccines.

Malaysia's Vaccine Portfolio

As part of the COVID-19 Vaccines Global Access (COVAX) initiative, Malaysia, as a self-financing participant, has purchased 6.6 million doses of vaccines manufactured by Oxford-AstraZeneca. Including self-purchases, Malaysia has purchased 66.7 million doses of Covid-19 vaccines covering 109.65% of the population.

As of 31 August 2021, Malaysia's National Immunisation Program (PICK) has been exclusively for those above 18 years old.

According to coordinating minister, Khairy Jamaluddin, those below 18 years old will only be inoculated after the adult population has been fully vaccinated in September .

Malaysia's vaccination rate picked up speed starting in June. Malaysia's inoculation rate has been one of the highest in the world in the past three months , surpsassing that of developed countries like Japan, Australia and New Zealand.

Is Malaysia's healthcare system still standing strong?

How many lives has Covid-19 claimed?

A major reason as to why governments around the world were quick to initiate nationwide lockdowns is to ensure the stability of their healthcare systems. Those severely infected with Covid-19 are at risk of being warded in intensive care units (ICUs) and may require ventilation if their conditions deteriorate rapidly. Death may even be a probable. Thus, lockdowns were declared everywhere to reduce the potential burden on the healthcare system.
